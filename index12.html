Language

I am sitting in Deventer at a café called the Seventh Heaven. In front of me I see the Lebuinus church, next to a large square that used to be a cemetery. I am sitting at a terrace outside; the pouring rain is dropping from the awning. The guy next to me lights his cigarette. A few men dressed in suits come out of the church. Next to the church a black funeral car is parked. Out of sight from the entrance of the church, around the corner the men in suits also light their cigarettes. The guy next to me is mumbling: ‘het is wel echt begrafenisweer’ (It is really the weather for a funeral). The rain keeps dropping and there is a deadly silence. I realize that the Dutch do not only have a huge amount of sayings to communicate weather conditions, but that we also have a huge amount of sayings in which death figures. Er heerst een doodse stilte (there is a deadly silence), het is echt begrafenisweer (it really is funeral weather), je ziet lijkbleek (you look pale as a dead body) en als de dood zijn voor iets (being deadly afraid of something).
